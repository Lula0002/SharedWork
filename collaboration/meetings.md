# Meetings

- Every meeting has an agenda shared before it starts.
- Decisions and action items are written down and owned by a named person.
- If a meeting has no agenda, it's okay to decline.

## Recurring formats

| Meeting | Cadence | Duration | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standup | Daily | 15 min | Surface blockers, align on the day |
| Sync | Weekly | 30 min | Unblock each other, surface risks |
| Retro | Monthly | 60 min | Improve how we work |

---

## Daily Standup

**Format:** Three questions, one answer each. In order, no crosstalk until everyone is done.

1. **What did I complete since last standup?**
2. **What will I work on today?**
3. **What is blocking me?**

### Rules

- Timebox to 15 minutes hard. If it runs over, parking lot the topic and continue after.
- Blockers are flagged here, not solved here. Take the conversation offline.
- If you're async / remote, post answers in the team channel before standup starts — don't skip the format.
- One person facilitates per week (rotating). Their job: keep time, call out parking lot items, close the meeting.

### What standup is not

- A status report to management.
- A place to debug or design.
- Optional — if you're blocked and don't surface it, that's on you.

### Parking lot

Items that come up but need more time get added to a shared parking lot list (bottom of the standup doc / channel thread). The facilitator is responsible for making sure each item gets an owner before the day ends.

---

## Retro

**Format:** Start / Stop / Continue

| Column | Question |
|---|---|
| **Start** | What should we begin doing that we aren't? |
| **Stop** | What are we doing that isn't adding value? |
| **Continue** | What's working well that we should keep? |

Each item gets a named owner and a deadline before the retro closes.
